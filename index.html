Contributing Factors to Australia's Aging Population

Increasing Life Expectancy

Life expectancy is the number of years a newborn is expected to live for. Factors such as health care access, lifestyle habits, and socioeconomic status can impact life expectancy. From 2000 to 2021, life expectancy has increased by 3.41 years from 79.7 years in 2000 to 83.1 years in 2021.

Lowering Fertility Rates

One of the primary drivers of an aging population is declining fertility rates. Over the past several decades, Australia has experienced a steady decline in the number of births per woman.

Tip: Hover over the graph to view the changes in birth rates over time.

In the 1960s, the fertility rate was around 3.5 children per woman, but today it is closer to 1.6.

This means fewer young people are entering the population, while older generations continue to grow. Lower fertility rates reduce the size of future working-age cohorts, exacerbating the imbalance between the elderly and the workforce.
